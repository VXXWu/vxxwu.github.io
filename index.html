ABOUT THE MAP

Social scientists deal with a lot of geographic information that is difficult to intuitively understand, but by utilizing visualization tools like interactive maps, geographic data can be examined in an easily processable format to develop new research questions and see relationships between metrics.

This interactive map can display a large dataset of over 30 different basic social demographic metrics, like sex and violent crime rate, that I gathered from various reliable sources, like the US Census Bureau and the FBI UCR (Uniform Crime Reporting Program), and can additionally display a dataset on crowdfunding gathered by a Stanford social scientist and processed by me. All of these metrics are listed below in several categories with some clarifying details. Enjoy!

TUTORIAL

1) Choose a general category of data that you want to visualize

2) Choose a specific metric within the category

3) Choose a year in which you want to visualize your specific demographic

4) Click the "Generate Map" button to generate an interactive map of your chosen demographic in your chosen year

You can change the category, specific demographic, or year at any time

ABOUT THE CREATOR

Hi there! My name is Vince Wu, and I am a high school freshman who made this map. I was inspired to create this map by accounts from social scientists of the difficulty of finding data on many different demographics and the inability to visualize a large amount of geographic data across multiple years. I hope my map can be an interesting and useful tool for you to use in visualizing geographic data.
